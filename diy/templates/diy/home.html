Time to digitalise SMEs in South Africa.

Covid-19 has forced SMEs to embrace technology!

The goal of implementing digital technology is no longer just to improve internal operations, but to expand internal dimensions, reach customers and external partners, affect services, integrate processes, disrupt markets, and fundamentally change industries, thanks to IT's unique features and accessibility. It's no surprise that the concept of digital transformation has sparked a lot of research interest, particularly among practitioners, but academic achievements have lagged behind, probably because digital transformation frameworks are still maturing. Digital transformation can be defined as a continual process of increasing digital maturity through the application of digital and other technology, as well as organizational practices, in order to establish a digital culture. In the end, this maturity will allow the organization to provide better services, obtain a competitive advantage, and respond to actions in a complicated environment more effectively. Companies that successfully implement digital transformation see higher asset returns and are more lucrative overall (Westerman et al., 2012).
